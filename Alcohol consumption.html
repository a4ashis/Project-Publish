How It's Calculated

This calculator determines the volume of your chosen drink that corresponds to the recommended daily limit for moderate consumption. The limit is based on "standard drinks":

  • Men: Up to 2 Standard Drinks per day.
  • Women: Up to 1 Standard Drink per day.

Formula:

Volume per Standard Drink (ml) = 17.74 / (Your Drink's ABV / 100)

This is derived from the fact that one standard drink contains 14g of pure alcohol (ethanol), and ethanol's density is 0.789 g/ml.

7 Key Principles for Safer Drinking

  • 1. Quantity is Paramount: The most critical factor. The more you drink, the higher the risk. Stick to the moderate limits calculated above.
  • 2. Choose Quality: Higher quality spirits often have fewer impurities (congeners), which can reduce the severity of hangovers. Avoid cheap or illicit alcohol.
  • 3. Understand ABV: A "drink" isn't standard. A 45ml shot of 40% whisky has the same alcohol as a 350ml glass of 5% beer. Know your drink's strength.
  • 4. Mind the Calories: Alcohol itself has 7 calories/gram. Your body can't use these for energy, so they're readily converted to fat, particularly harmful visceral fat.
  • 5. Avoid Cocktails & Mixers: Sugary mixers (juice, soda) can double the calories of a drink, overload the liver, and make you crave more. They are a primary cause of "beer belly" and weight gain.
  • 6. Eat First, Hydrate Always: Never drink on an empty stomach. A meal with protein and healthy fats slows alcohol absorption. Drink a glass of water for every alcoholic drink to combat dehydration.
  • 7. Don't Drink Alone: Drinking is a social activity. Using it as a coping mechanism while alone can lead to dependency and is linked with poorer health outcomes.

Beer (regular) 5% ~350 ml 150-170 kcal High in carbs; contains gluten. Very calorie-dense.
