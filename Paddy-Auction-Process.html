Introduction

The Chhattisgarh State Cooperative Marketing Federation (CGMARKFED) conducts e-auctions for the sale of paddy through mjunction services Limited's e-auction platform. This comprehensive guide provides potential bidders with all the necessary information required to successfully participate in the auction process.

Eligibility Criteria

The e-auction is open to a wide range of participants as detailed below:

Eligible Entities

  • Individuals
  • Firms
  • Societies
  • Companies
  • Joint Ventures
  • Limited-Liability Partnerships (LLPs)

Required Documents

  • Audited Annual Reports (latest 3 years) or Provisional Financial Statements for new firms
  • Income Tax Returns (latest 3 years)
  • PAN Card copy
  • GST Registration Certificate
  • Authorized Signatory Documentation (for partnerships/companies)
  • Identity and Address Proof of Authorized Signatory

Registration Process

Bidders must first self-register on the designated portal: cgmarkfed.mjunction.in

All submitted documents must be self-attested.

Registration remains open for the entire selling season.

Pro Tip

Begin the registration process early to ensure all documentation is properly verified before auction announcements. This gives you adequate time to address any discrepancies in your application.

Process Timeline

Understanding the chronological flow of the e-auction process is crucial for successful participation:

Registration

Complete online registration on cgmarkfed.mjunction.in portal. Registration stays open throughout the selling season.

Document Submission

Upload all required documents for verification. All documents must be self-attested.

EMD Deposit

Deposit the Earnest Money Deposit (1% of acquisition cost) prior to participating in any auction.

Auction Participation

Bid during the scheduled auction time as announced on CGMARKFED website and mjunction portal.

Bid Confirmation

CGMARKFED confirms the highest valid bid after the auction closes.

Security Deposit

Successful bidders must deposit the Security Deposit (3% of acquisition cost) within 7 working days of bid confirmation.

Buyer Order Issuance

Buyer Order (BO) is issued after Security Deposit payment.

Full Payment

Complete payment for the trade value within the specified timeline based on stack quantity.

Delivery Order

Delivery Order (DO) is issued after receiving full payment.

Lifting of Paddy

Lifting must be completed within the specified timeline based on stack quantity.

Financial Requirements

Understanding the financial obligations is essential before participating in the e-auction:

Deposits Required

  • Earnest Money Deposit (EMD):
    • 1% of acquisition cost
    • Must be deposited before bidding
    • Refundable for unsuccessful bidders
  • Security Deposit:
    • 3% of acquisition cost
    • Must be deposited within 7 working days after bid confirmation

Additional Charges

  • Transaction Fee:
    • 0.01% of the total traded value (inclusive of GST)
    • Payable to mjunction services Limited
  • Tax Deduction:
    • TDS of 0.1% under section 194-O
    • Deducted from the trade amount
  • Weighing Charges:
    • Cost to be borne by the bidder/buyer
    • Using CGMARKFED authorized weighbridge

Calculation Example

Scenario: Bidding for a stack containing 10,000 quintals of paddy at Rs. 2,100 per quintal

Total Acquisition Cost 10,000 quintals × Rs. 2,100 = Rs. 2,10,00,000
EMD Required (1%) Rs. 2,10,000
Security Deposit (3%) Rs. 6,30,000
Transaction Fee (0.01%) Rs. 2,100
TDS (0.1%) Rs. 21,000
Total Immediate Financial Requirement EMD + Security Deposit = Rs. 8,40,000

Important Financial Notes

  • Ensure sufficient funds are available in your account for both EMD and Security Deposit.
  • EMD is refundable for unsuccessful bidders but forfeited for non-compliance with terms by successful bidders.
  • Late payments may incur penalties (Rs. 1 per quintal per day) up to a maximum period of one month.
  • Price quotation is on net weight basis (excluding bag weight).

Bidding Process

A thorough understanding of the bidding process will enable you to participate effectively in the e-auction:

Bidding Method

  • Bids must be placed online via cgmarkfed.mjunction.in portal
  • Entire stack must be bid for (no partial bidding allowed)
  • Bids quoted in Rs/Quintal, inclusive of paddy and gunny bags price
  • Net weight basis (excluding bag weight)
  • Incremental bidding with potential time extensions
  • Bid remains valid for 15 working days from auction completion

Auction Dynamics

  • Dynamic auction with possible time extensions
  • If bids received in last 3 minutes, auction extends by 5 minutes
  • Maximum of 3 extensions possible
  • Highest valid bid (H-1) is determined by CGMARKFED
  • CGMARKFED reserves the right to accept or reject any bid
  • No bid cancellation allowed once placed

Step-by-Step Bidding Process

  1. Pre-Auction Preparation:
    • Complete registration and document verification
    • Deposit the required EMD amount
    • Check auction schedule on CGMARKFED website or mjunction portal
  2. Auction Participation:
    • Log in to the portal at the scheduled time
    • Review available stacks and their details
    • Place bids in Rs/Quintal for selected stacks
    • Monitor the auction for competitive bids
    • Place higher bids if necessary before auction close/extension
  3. Post-Auction Process:
    • Wait for bid confirmation from CGMARKFED
    • Deposit Security Deposit within 7 working days if successful
    • Receive Buyer Order (BO) after Security Deposit payment
    • Complete full payment within specified timeline
    • Receive Delivery Order (DO) to proceed with lifting

Auction Amendments

The recent corrigendum has specified that bid price must be quoted on delivered weight and quality basis on an 'as is where is' basis. Price quotation is in Rs/Quintal including both paddy and gunny bags price.

Strategic Bidding Tips

  • Research market prices thoroughly before bidding
  • Set a maximum bid limit based on your financial capacity and business model
  • Be prepared for last-minute extensions with pre-determined bid increments
  • Consider the logistics and lifting costs when calculating your maximum bid amount

Delivery Terms

Understanding the delivery process and terms is crucial for efficient lifting of the purchased paddy:

Delivery Process

  1. Complete full payment for the won stacks
  2. Receive Buyer Order (BO) after Security Deposit payment
  3. Receive Delivery Order (DO) after full payment
  4. Present DO at the stock location
  5. Arrange transportation and labor for lifting
  6. Complete the weighing process at authorized weighbridge
  7. Complete lifting within the specified timeline

Lifting Timelines

Number of Stacks Lifting Period
Up to 2 stacks 1 week
Up to 5 stacks 2 weeks
More than 5 stacks 3 weeks

*Timeline starts from the issuance of buyer/delivery order

Weighing and Quality Considerations

  • Delivery is on net weight basis (excluding bag weight)
  • Weighing must be done at CGMARKFED authorized/identified weighbridge
  • Weighing is the responsibility of the bidder/buyer
  • All weighing costs are to be borne by the bidder/buyer
  • Sale is on "as is where is" basis with no subsequent quality claims entertained
  • All loading charges and other delivery-related costs are borne by the buyer

Important Delivery Notes

  • Failure to lift within the specified timeline may result in penalties (Rs. 1 per quintal per day)
  • Maximum penalty period is one month, after which CGMARKFED may take other action
  • Buyer must arrange adequate transportation in advance to meet lifting deadlines
  • Ensure proper coordination with the storage location for smooth lifting process

Delivery Planning Tips

  • Establish contact with the storage location staff in advance
  • Prepare a daily lifting schedule to ensure completion within the timeline
  • Arrange for multiple transportation vehicles if lifting large quantities
  • Keep contingency plans for potential delays due to weather or other factors
  • Maintain proper documentation of all lifted quantities and weighbridge receipts

Risks and Challenges

Being aware of potential risks and challenges will help bidders prepare adequately:

Financial Risks

  • Forfeiture of EMD if payment terms are not met
  • Forfeiture of Security Deposit for non-compliance
  • Penalties for delayed lifting (Rs. 1 per quintal per day)
  • Market price fluctuations affecting profitability
  • Unforeseen expenses in transportation and logistics

Operational Risks

  • Quality issues due to 'as is where is' purchase condition
  • Challenges in arranging adequate transportation
  • Difficulties in meeting lifting deadlines
  • Weather-related complications during lifting
  • Technical failures during online bidding process

Risk Mitigation Strategies

CGMARKFED Discretionary Powers

Be aware that CGMARKFED reserves the right to:

  • Accept or reject any bid at its sole discretion
  • Cancel trades in case of non-compliance
  • Modify auction terms through corrigenda
  • Take action against defaulters beyond financial penalties

Tips for Successful Bidding

Strategic approaches and best practices to enhance your chances of successful bidding and execution:

Pre-Auction Research

  • Study market trends and paddy prices
  • Research storage locations and quality history
  • Analyze transportation costs from various locations
  • Understand paddy varieties and their market demand
  • Review previous auction results if available

During Auction

  • Log in 30 minutes before scheduled time
  • Have all reference materials ready
  • Set clear maximum bid limits for each stack
  • Be prepared for time extensions
  • Stay calm and avoid emotional bidding
  • Consider stack location and quality in bidding

Post-Auction Execution

  • Promptly arrange for Security Deposit
  • Prepare lifting schedule immediately
  • Secure transportation arrangements in advance
  • Maintain proper documentation
  • Coordinate with storage location personnel
  • Monitor quality during lifting process

Financial Strategy Tips

  1. Cost-Plus Bidding Approach:

    Calculate backward from your selling price, accounting for all costs including transportation, processing, and profit margin to determine your maximum bid.

  2. Tiered Bidding Strategy:

    Prepare different bid levels based on quality expectations and stack locations to quickly adjust during auction.

  3. Cash Flow Management:

    Ensure sufficient funds are available not just for EMD and Security Deposit, but also for full payment and operational expenses.

  4. Risk Assessment:

    Factor in potential penalties, quality variations, and market fluctuations when setting your bid price.

  5. Volume Strategy:

    Consider bidding for multiple smaller stacks rather than fewer large ones to manage lifting logistics more effectively.

Quality Considerations

  • Understand the different paddy sub-varieties (MOTA, PATLA, SARNA) and their market values
  • Consider moisture content implications on weight and quality
  • Factor in potential foreign matter content when calculating yield
  • Research processing requirements for different varieties

Expert Insights

  • Develop relationships with storage location managers for better coordination
  • Network with transportation providers in advance for better rates
  • Consider consortium bidding for larger quantities if permitted
  • Maintain detailed records of all transactions for future reference and analysis
  • Analyze seasonal patterns in paddy quality and prices for strategic bidding
